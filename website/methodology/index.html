METHODOLOGY

How our pre-feasibility engine works

The data sources, conservatism rules, risk framework, and scope boundaries behind every CarbonRoots output.

CarbonRoots is a pre-feasibility engine for afforestation, reforestation, and revegetation (ARR) carbon projects. It gives developers and investors a disciplined, sourced, early-stage view of carbon yield, commercial viability, methodology fit, and risk — the stage before a project commits capital to a full Project Design Document.

This page documents exactly where our numbers come from, how we handle uncertainty, and the honest limits of what pre-feasibility can and cannot tell you.

Data hierarchy

Every parameter the engine uses resolves through a deliberate hierarchy. The tier actually applied for every variable is logged on each run, so any number in your output is reproducible and has a known provenance.

  1. User-provided input — your data takes precedence wherever available.
  2. Regional benchmark preset — country × land-type × parameter, calibrated against regional literature and versioned with a review owner and last-reviewed date.
  3. IPCC 2006 AFOLU default or FAO regional synthesis — applied when site-specific data is absent.
  4. Conservative proxy — a cautious fallback, flagged as low confidence in your output so you know it's a proxy rather than site-specific science.

Conservatism

When published literature gives a range, we use the midpoint or the lower third — never the upper bound. IPCC defaults serve as a floor rather than a ceiling. Early-stage projects carry more unknowns than mature ones, and optimism at pre-feasibility costs developers capital and credibility downstream. A conservative number that survives due diligence is worth more than an optimistic number that doesn't.

Risk framework

The engine applies a five-vector risk adjustment. Each vector is rated Low, Medium, or High, and mapped to explicit numerical adjustments — not a single blended buffer number.

Risk vectorWhat it adjusts
BiologicalYield multiplier and survival
DisturbanceCredit retention, issuance delay, volatility
BaselineCredit discount
ImplementationEstablishment factor and leakage
MRV / dataConfidence reporting only — never numeric outputs

Separating these vectors matters because the interventions that address them are different. A biological risk is closed by better species selection; an implementation risk is closed by a stronger on-the-ground partner. Lumping them into a single buffer number hides the actual levers a project has.

P50 and P90 risk-adjusted scenarios are produced alongside the base case, in line with investor due-diligence convention.

Uncertainty

A 10% uncertainty haircut is applied to deterministic credits on top of the risk framework, to reflect data gaps, growth-model simplification, and implementation variability.

Every parameter on every run is tagged with a confidence score — High, Medium, or Low. Every data gap is surfaced as a flag in your output. If a number is a conservative proxy rather than site-specific data, the output tells you so in the same place the number appears.

Transparency

Four features make every output auditable:

  • Source IDs on every biomass and regional parameter, traceable to the originating reference
  • Evidence versioning — country parameter sets carry a version tag, review owner, and last-reviewed date
  • QA gates on every run covering formula errors, IRR consistency, and issuance-basis reconciliation, producing a PASS / REVIEW / FAIL status
  • Visible low-confidence flags — proxy values and data gaps are surfaced in the output itself, not buried in footnotes

What CarbonRoots is not

CarbonRoots is a screening-level decision-support tool. It is explicitly not:

  • A substitute for formal validation or verification under any carbon standard
  • A registry submission, approval, or listing
  • Investment advice, a security, or a financial recommendation
  • A guarantee of credit issuance, carbon price, or project returns

The accounting scaffolding — baseline deduction, leakage, buffer, risk-adjusted issuance — mirrors how registries treat credits, so numbers translate cleanly when you move to a full Project Design Document. But a pre-feasibility screen is designed to tell you whether to take a project to validation, not to replace validation itself.
