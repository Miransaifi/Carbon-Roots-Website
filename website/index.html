Carbon Roots engine

A modular pre-feasibility workflow built for multiple carbon pathways.

  1. 01

    Intake

    Standardize core project inputs, decision context, and data quality. Flag gaps early.

  2. 02

    Feasibility framing

    Define baseline logic and pathway assumptions based on selected stream (ARR, Biochar, REDD+, or other NBS).

  3. 03

    Carbon estimation

    Estimate indicative carbon potential using pathway-specific factors and conservative defaults where needed.

  4. 04

    Risk adjustments

    Apply risk vectors, uncertainty adjustments, and scenario logic (base, P50, P90 style) to stress-test outputs.

  5. 05

    Financial modelling

    Translate carbon outputs into revenue scenarios with NPV, IRR, payback, and sensitivity views.

  6. 06

    Investment screen

    Classify outcomes as investable, conditionally viable, or not yet ready based on readiness and risk profile.

  7. 07

    Output

    Deliver clear recommendations, flagged data gaps, and practical next-step pathways.

Decision brief

Project classification:

      TRUST AND CREDIBILITY

      Methodology-aware, robustly structured, and built for early-stage decisions.

      • Grounded in IPCC 2006 AFOLU, FAO, World Agroforestry, and CIFOR regional literature — carbon accounting fundamentals, species growth curves, and biomass defaults come from the same data hierarchy carbon registries rely on.
      • Stress-tested with a five-vector risk framework and P50/P90 scenarios — biological, disturbance, baseline, implementation, and MRV risks modelled independently; outputs use conservative midpoints, never literature maxima.
      • Every parameter carries a source ID, confidence score, and data-gap flag — if a number is a conservative proxy rather than site-specific data, your output tells you so.

      Validation approach

      How robustness is built in

      Carbon accounting follows IPCC 2006 AFOLU (carbon fraction 0.47, 44/12 CO₂ conversion, species-specific root-to-shoot ratios). Baseline, leakage, and buffer deductions follow Verra VCS conventions. A 10% uncertainty haircut applies on top of deterministic credits, with P50 and P90 risk-adjusted scenarios alongside the base case.

      Scope boundary

      This remains a screening-level decision-support tool, not validation, verification, registry approval, investment advice, or guaranteed returns.

      Primary data sources referenced in our engine:

      IPCC · FAO · World Agroforestry (ICRAF) · CIFOR · World Bank · Verra VCS framework
